How does it work?

The pythagorean theorem is a proven theorem used to mesure the sides and type of a triangle. You have the legs, A and B, and the hypothenuse, C.

To find the type of triangle:

Here instead of solving to find a value, you compare them. You fist simplify all the values as explained previously then add them all up and compare them. If they are equal, the triangle is a right triangle otherwise, it is a obtuse or acute triangle.
